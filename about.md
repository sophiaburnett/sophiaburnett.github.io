I am a PhD candidate in language sciences at CY Cergy Paris Université. My doctoral dissertation concerns discourse analysis, in particular the semantics of non-standard typography in online discourse, and the sociophonetic enregisterments and indexicalities of those non-standard forms. My current research also investigates typographic variants as embodied linguistic markers of depression and anorexia nervosa. While French and other romance languages are my natural go-to for cross-linguistic analyses, I am looking to explore other writing systems than our little latin alphabet. This year I am a nomadic dissertation writer and builder’s tea drinker, dividing my time between charming hosts in Edinburgh, Paris, and Manchester.
