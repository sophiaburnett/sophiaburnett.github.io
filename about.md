I am a PhD candidate in language sciences at [CY Cergy Paris Université](https://cyagora.cyu.fr/) under the supervision of [Julien Longhi](https://cv.hal.science/julien-longhi). My doctoral dissertation concerns discourse analysis, in particular the semantics of [non-standard typography in online text discourse](https://www.degruyter.com/document/doi/10.1515/eujal-2022-0012/html), and the sociophonetic enregisterments and indexicalities of those non-standard forms. My doctoral  research also investigates typographic variants as embodied linguistic markers of depression and [anorexia nervosa](https://amsacta.unibo.it/id/eprint/7248/). While French and other romance languages are my natural go-to for cross-linguistic analyses, I am looking to explore other writing systems than our little latin script. The methods employed in order to comprehend the use and perception of these variants include for example, computational linguistics &mdash; using textometry software such as TXM and Iramuteq for corpus analysis, acoustic phonetics analysis with Praat, and novel surveys to test my hypotheses and predictions.  \#discourse_analysis \#sociophonetics  \#indexicality  \#cognitive linguistics  \#linguistic_anthropology   \#linguistic_relativity
