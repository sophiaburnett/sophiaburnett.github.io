## Sophia Burnett

I am a PhD candidate in language sciences at CY Cergy Paris Université with a particular interest in non-standard typography in online discourse, and the sociophonetic enregisterments and indexicalities of non-standard forms. My research also investigates typographic variants as embodied linguistic markers of depression and anorexia nervosa. While French and other romance languages are my natural go-to for cross-linguistic analyses, I am looking to explore other writing systems than our little latin alphabet.
My research uses any qualitative and quantitative methods that shine light upon sign variation, including lexical statistics, textometry, and acoustic phonetics. I am interested in understanding how language is used in computer-mediated communication, as well as how written linguistic features may provide insight into psychopathologies and somatic conditions.
This year I am a nomadic dissertation writer and heavy tea drinker, dividing my time between Edinburgh, Paris, and Manchester.
