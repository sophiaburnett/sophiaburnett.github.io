My Ph.D thesis "White Flag Typography" under the supervision of Julien Longhi was successfully defended at CY Cergy Paris University on December 1st, 2023. The thesis concerns discourse analysis in Computer Mediated Communications, and proposes a taxonomy of 6 usages of non-standard minuscules, in particular the non-standard 1SG. It draws on a wide interdisciplinary and theoretical base, applying notions such as indexicality, enregisterment, and iconicity, and combining them with elements of cognitive theory and social psychology to provide a multifacetted yet deeply analysed ‘profile’ for each of the six usages. While French and other romance languages are my natural go-to for cross-linguistic analyses, I am looking to explore other writing systems than our little latin script. This research also investigates graphetic/typographic variants as embodied linguistic markers of depression and [anorexia nervosa](https://amsacta.unibo.it/id/eprint/7248/). The interdisciplinary nature of my research is reflected the range of experimental methods employed in order to comprehend the use and perception of these variants, for example: computational linguistics &mdash; textometry software such as [TXM](https://txm.gitpages.huma-num.fr/textometrie/files/software/TXM/0.8.2/welcome/) and [Iramuteq](http://www.iramuteq.org/) for corpus analysis, acoustic phonetics analysis with [Praat](https://www.fon.hum.uva.nl/praat/), and novel surveys to test my hypotheses and predictions.
