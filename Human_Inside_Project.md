As a language scientist and linguistic anthropologist, I am fascinated by how humans use text to communicate, particularly on Computer Mediated Communications (CMC) such as social media. I feel it is important to conduct research that addresses, analyses and documents how humans write to each other, especially in an age where we run the risk of losing sight of what defines human written production, when it is digitally mediated. As many quite naturally turn to AI for help in editorial chores, the internet is gradually filling up with text that originated in a black box of Large Language Models, not a human mind. 
I do think AI has the potential to provide amazing assistance to humanity, I also think a lot has been deployed without forethought, and we need to keep tabs on what makes us human in our language. The full Human Inside Project has a citizen science component and is ambitious in scope. I hope to acquire funding to deploy the subsequent parts of the projects, which include explorations of linguistic embodiment. For now I am pleased to begin part one. <br>
### [**The Human Inside Project**](https://sites.google.com/view/humaninside/home) Part 1 is a citizen science project that will rely on crowdsourced data. Please do participate, it takes between 15 and 30 minutes to complete.<br>
