I am a PhD candidate in language sciences at CY Cergy Paris Université. My doctoral dissertation concerns discourse analysis, in particular the semantics of non-standard typography in online text discourse, and the sociophonetic enregisterments and indexicalities of those non-standard forms. My current research also investigates typographic variants as embodied linguistic markers of depression and anorexia nervosa. While French and other romance languages are my natural go-to for cross-linguistic analyses, I am looking to explore other writing systems than our little latin alphabet. My approach in order to comrehend the use and perception of these variants is holistic; drawing on computational linguistics (textometry) for corpus analysis, acoustic phonetics analysis (such as Praat), and novel surveys to test my theory and hypotheses. \#discourse_analysis \#sociophonetics  \#indexicality  \#cognitive linguistics  \#linguistic_anthropology
