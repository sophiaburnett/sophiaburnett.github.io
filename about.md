I am a PhD candidate in language sciences at CY Cergy Paris Université. My doctoral dissertation concerns discourse analysis, in particular the semantics of non-standard typography in online text discourse, and the sociophonetic enregisterments and indexicalities of those non-standard forms. This research also investigates typographic variants as embodied linguistic markers of depression and anorexia nervosa. While French and other romance languages are my natural go-to for cross-linguistic analyses, I am looking to explore other writing systems than our little latin alphabet. The methods employed in order to comprehend the use and perception of these variants includes for example, computational linguistics &mdash; using textometry software such as TXM and Iramuteq for corpus analysis, acoustic phonetics analysis with Praat, and novel surveys to test my hypotheses and predictions.  \#discourse_analysis \#sociophonetics  \#indexicality  \#cognitive linguistics  \#linguistic_anthropology   \#linguistic_relativity
