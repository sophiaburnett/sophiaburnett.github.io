Most of my work looks at the strategies users deploy to imbue signs with sense, and our cultural, sociological, and cognitive priors that allow them to do so.[White Flag Typography](https://sophiaburnett.github.io/Burnett_White%20Flag%20Typography_2024.pdf) my Ph.D thesis supervised by [Julien Longhi](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=KKt7T4kAAAAJ&hl=fr), was successfully defended at CY Cergy Paris University in December. The thesis concerns discourse analysis in Computer Mediated Communications, and proposes a typology of 6 usages of non-standard minuscules, in particular the non-standard 1SG. It draws on a wide interdisciplinary and theoretical base, applying notions such as prosody, indexicality, enregisterment, and iconicity, and combining them with elements of cognitive theory and social psychology to provide a usage profile for each of the six taxa. This research included the study of the phenomenon's potential embodied and physiological isomorphisms of depression and of [anorexia nervosa](https://doi.org/10.31219/osf.io/gz72t).<br>
