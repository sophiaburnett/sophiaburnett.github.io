I am a [PhD candidate]( https://xkcd.com/285/) in language sciences at [CY Cergy Paris Université](https://cyagora.cyu.fr/) under the supervision of [Julien Longhi](https://cv.hal.science/julien-longhi). My doctoral dissertation concerns discourse analysis, in particular the semantics of [non-standard typography in online text discourse](https://www.degruyter.com/document/doi/10.1515/eujal-2022-0012/html), and the sociophonetic enregisterments and indexicalities of those non-standard forms. While French and other romance languages are my natural go-to for cross-linguistic analyses, I am looking to explore other writing systems than our little latin script. This research also investigates typographic variants as embodied linguistic markers of depression and [anorexia nervosa](https://amsacta.unibo.it/id/eprint/7248/). My research is interdisciplinary, and this is reflected the range of empirical methods employed in order to comprehend the use and perception of these variants, for example: computational linguistics &mdash; using textometry software such as [TXM](https://txm.gitpages.huma-num.fr/textometrie/files/software/TXM/0.8.2/welcome/) and [Iramuteq](http://www.iramuteq.org/) for corpus analysis, acoustic phonetics analysis with [Praat](https://www.fon.hum.uva.nl/praat/), and novel surveys to test my hypotheses and predictions.
